The goal: For a function \(f:\mathbb{N} \to \mathbb{N}\) of the form \[ f = \begin{cases} \sum\limits_{i = 0}^n a_ix^i & x \equiv 1 \pmod 2 \\ x / c & x \equiv 0 \pmod 2, \end{cases} \] where \(a_i \in \mathbb{N}\) for \(i = 1,\dots,n\), determine whether or not there exists \(x \in \mathbb{N}\) and distinct \(k,m \in \mathbb{N}\) such that \(f^k(x) = f^m(x)\). For the time being, we are concerned with cases where \(n = 1\) and \(c = 2\).

What is characteristic about problems of this nature is the removal of all factors of \(c\) from \(x\). In the future, we might perhaps consider functions over different moduli and with \(c\) being an integer greater than 2.
